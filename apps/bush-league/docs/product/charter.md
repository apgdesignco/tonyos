# Bush League Product Charter

## Vision
Create the most approachable deep baseball organization simulator: a game where players can understand their choices, grow attached to fictional athletes and staff, and shape a franchise over decades without fighting a clumsy interface.

## Mission
Combine roster construction, player development, scouting, ballpark operations, finances, fan culture, and league history into one coherent management experience. Support both short sessions and long careers, allowing players to decide how closely they follow each game.

## Core Player Fantasy
> Build a small baseball organization into a living institution with its own stars, failures, traditions, rivalries, ballpark, and history.

## Problem
Sports management games often split into deep but intimidating simulations or accessible mobile games with shallow, fragmented, or grind-heavy systems. Bush League aims to preserve meaningful simulation while making the organization understandable and emotionally engaging.

## Target Players
- Sports-management players who enjoy rosters, prospects, drafts, trades, statistics, and long-term simulation.
- Baseball fans intimidated by traditional simulation software.
- Cozy and indie-management players attracted to character and gradual growth.
- Systems-minded players who prefer operating an organization to controlling athletes directly.

## Product Pillars
1. Decisions over navigation.
2. Friendly depth.
3. A living, interconnected organization.
4. Flexible simulation pacing.
5. Long-term attachment and persistent history.
6. Character-rich, low-poly presentation.
7. Honest progression without pay-to-win or artificial energy systems.

## First Vertical Slice
1. Open the organization dashboard.
2. Review team condition and priorities.
3. Set or adjust a lineup.
4. Simulate one game.
5. Save the result, box score, player statistics, fatigue, and standings.
6. Present a readable recap.
7. Advance to the next day.

## Initial Success Criteria
- A new player understands what requires attention.
- The daily loop can be completed in a few minutes.
- Repeated simulations preserve valid league state.
- Consequences are explained rather than hidden.
- Fictional players begin to feel memorable.
- Saved leagues load reliably.

## Initial Non-Goals
- Licensed MLB teams or players.
- Real-time 3D match presentation.
- Multiplayer leagues.
- A complete 162-game rules simulation in the first build.
- Generative AI as a dependency for core gameplay.
- Mobile monetization systems during the learning prototype.
