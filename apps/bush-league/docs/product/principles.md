# Bush League Product Principles

These principles guide product, design, engineering, and scope decisions.

1. **Player decisions matter more than grinding.**
2. **The simulation should be believable before it is exhaustive.**
3. **Statistics should explain stories, not merely fill tables.**
4. **Every screen should answer a meaningful organizational question.**
5. **Short sessions should still feel productive.**
6. **Complexity must be earned through clear layers and progressive disclosure.**
7. **Fast simulation is always available.**
8. **Offline and reliable save play are first-class concerns.**
9. **Features must interact with the larger organization.**
10. **No pay-to-win, artificial energy limits, or manipulative grind.**
11. **AI may assist development and optional presentation, but core systems remain deterministic, testable, and understandable.**
12. **Documentation, requirements, architecture decisions, tests, and implementation evolve together.**

## Decision Test

Before adding a feature, ask:

- Does it strengthen the fantasy of running a baseball organization?
- Does it create a meaningful decision or useful understanding?
- Does it connect to another system?
- Can a player understand its consequences?
- Can we test it reliably?
- Is it necessary now, or merely interesting later?
